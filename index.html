2. Not Understanding Your Preventive Care Coverage

Most health insurance plans cover preventive care at 100%, meaning you pay nothing out of pocket for annual physicals, certain screenings, and vaccinations. Yet studies show that nearly 40% of Americans skip their annual checkup, often because they assume it will cost them money.

By not taking advantage of free preventive care, you're missing opportunities to catch health issues early when they're easier and cheaper to treat. The same applies to dental insurance—most plans cover two cleanings per year at no cost to you. If you're not scheduling these appointments, you're essentially paying for a service you're not using.

3. Staying with Out-of-Network Providers

Using out-of-network doctors and dentists can cost you significantly more in copays and deductibles. Many people stick with a provider they've used for years without realizing they're paying extra because that provider is no longer in their insurance network.

Before your next appointment, take a few minutes to verify that your provider is still in-network. If they're not, consider switching to an in-network provider. The savings can be substantial, especially if you need multiple visits or procedures throughout the year.

Platforms like Opencare make it easy to find top-rated, in-network dentists in your area, ensuring you get quality care without paying out-of-network premiums.

4. Ignoring Your FSA or HSA Funds

Flexible Spending Accounts (FSAs) often have a "use it or lose it" rule at the end of the year. If you have money sitting in your FSA, now is the time to use it on eligible expenses like dental work, glasses, contact lenses, or even sunscreen.

Health Savings Accounts (HSAs) do roll over, but many people forget they have them or don't realize how many expenses qualify. Dental cleanings, exams, and treatments are all FSA and HSA eligible, making it an excellent way to use those funds before they expire.

Check your FSA or HSA balance today and schedule that dental appointment you've been putting off. You're literally using your own pre-tax money that will disappear if you don't act.

5. Not Appealing Denied Claims

Insurance companies deny claims all the time, but many of these denials can be successfully appealed. Most people simply accept the denial and pay out of pocket, not realizing they have the right to challenge the decision.

If your insurance denies a claim, read the explanation of benefits carefully to understand why. Often, it's a simple coding error or missing documentation that can be corrected. Contact your provider's billing department and your insurance company to start the appeals process.

Studies show that a significant percentage of appealed claims are eventually approved. Don't leave money on the table just because the first answer was no.

6. Skipping Generic Medications

Brand-name medications can cost significantly more than their generic equivalents, which contain the same active ingredients and are held to the same FDA standards. Always ask your doctor if a generic option is available for any prescription.

In many cases, your insurance will cover generics at a much lower copay or even for free, while brand-name drugs might require you to pay a significant portion out of pocket. The savings can add up to hundreds or even thousands of dollars per year, especially for medications you take regularly. It's best to consult professionals in regards to oral health because restorative services can get costly.

7. Paying for Services Already Covered

Some people pay out of pocket for services like flu shots, wellness visits, or routine screenings without realizing their insurance covers them for free. Always check your benefits summary before paying for any preventive service.

Under the Affordable Care Act, most insurance plans are required to cover a wide range of preventive services at no cost to you. This includes annual wellness visits, certain cancer screenings, immunizations, and preventive dental care.

Before you pay for any medical or dental service, call your insurance company or check your online portal to confirm whether it's covered. You might be surprised at how many services are included in your plan at no additional cost.

8. Not Shopping Around for Better Rates

Insurance premiums and out-of-pocket costs can vary widely between providers and plans. If you haven't reviewed your coverage options in a few years, you might be paying more than necessary.

During open enrollment, take the time to compare different plans and providers. Look at the total cost of coverage, including premiums, deductibles, and copays, not just the monthly premium. Sometimes a plan with a slightly higher premium can save you money overall if it has lower out-of-pocket costs for the services you use most.

The same applies to finding healthcare providers. Shopping around for dentists, doctors, and specialists can help you find quality care at better prices, especially if you're willing to switch to an in-network provider.
